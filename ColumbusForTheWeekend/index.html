History of Columbus, Ohio

Columbus was founded on February 14, 1812 as the start of a new state capital set in the center of Ohio. The choice was favorable because of its location near major transportation routes at the time - primarily rivers. The railroad came to town by 1830, cementing Columbus' position in history. By virtue of the Morrill Land-Grant Colleges Act, the Ohio Agricultural and Mechanical College (which became The Ohio State University) founded in 1870 on the former estate of William and Hannah Neil.
Columbus has had many aliases, including Cowtown and Cbus. Arch City, an 1890s nickname, stemmed from the city's construction of arches over key streets. The arches provided power to the city's new electric streetcars.
Today, Columbus is the 14th largest city in America. The diverse population ensures that there is always something new to check out.
