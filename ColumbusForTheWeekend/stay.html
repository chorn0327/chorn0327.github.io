Friday

Sometimes the stresses of school work are too much. Sometimes you're in Columbus alone for a weekend and you aren't sure what to do. In these times, you need a staycation.

When I'm keeping it lowkey, pizza is usually the go to. Cicilia's (conveniently located inside of Out-R-Inn) is a great spot to grab a quick slice that'll really hit the spot.

Saturday

Pistacia Vera is a boutique coffee and pastry spot in the German Village. If you happen to visit by yourself, there's no one to judge you if you indulge in a sweet (or three, they have an amazing selection!)

While in the German Village, stop by the Book Loft. This is a bibliophile's paradine, offering 32 ROOMS of books on display. If you can't find anything to read here, consider podcasts.

While you're treating yourself, swing by Hot Chicken Takeover in Clintonville. You get to pick the spice level for the chicken and the mac and cheese is insane.

The Old North has several thrift stores to shop at, you're certain to find a funky new addition to your wardrobe while wandering around.

Sunday

Mornings are for coffee and contemplation. Therefore, you'd better get yourself to Fox in the Snow for plenty of both as they don't offer wifi. They do offer the best breakfast sandwich in Columbus though so it's a good trade off.

If you're in the mood to windowshop, the Short North has a variety of fun shops to look through. One of my personal favorites is Tigertree, which is always eclectic and usually fairly on trend as well.

On a beautiful day, a walk through an old Columbus neighborhood is always the move. Columbus has a great deal of curb appeal to explore, you never know what you might find.
