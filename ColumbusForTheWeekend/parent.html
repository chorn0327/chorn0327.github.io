Parental/Guardian

Friday

If you really want to impress your visiting parents, make them dinner. I know, this isn't technically a recommendation of where to go visit in Columbus but it's good advice. They'll be so proud that they raised a competent human being, it's a good way to start the weekend on a great note.

After dinner, catching a movie is usually a popular choice. Columbus has several smaller theaters that have great charm, and are often playing the recent releases as well as throw back offerings. Grand View Theater and Drafthouse is a great choice that isn't too far from campus.

Saturday

One Line Coffee in the short north offers a variety of coffee options that are sure to please everyone in your family. Set in a nice establishment with ample parking, it's a great place to grab a cup and relax.

Once you have your coffee fix, you're ready to go and get to shopping in the Short North. Especially around the holidays, the Short North is a unique place for your family to browse and find something neat for presents.

Brassica has a variety of fresh and flavorful options for your family to pick from in a quickserve format. The falafel is incredible and the french fries are pretty darn good as well.

Take your parents on a walking tour, especially showing them your classroom building and the library. Especially if your parents happen to be alumni of Ohio State, they'll love visiting your favorite haunts and it's a nice time to tell them about your current interests and talk about your classes.

Cap City Diner is a neat restaurant to have dinner at with your parents. It's styled after an old fashioned diner, full of nostalgic artifacts, and serves a pretty decent milkshake as well.

Sunday

The best way to top off a weekend with your family is with a nice scoop of Jeni's. With their eclectic flavors, you will certainly please everyone.

The Columbus Museum of Art offers free admission on Sundays, make sure to stop by and see the Lego sculpture of Columbus on the first floor. There is a wide range of art here from pre-historic all the way to modern, a must see.
